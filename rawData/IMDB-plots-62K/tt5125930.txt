Shattered after the brutal assault by two rapists in her house, a young mother of two decides to keep her ordeal secret from her unsuspecting husband.
After being violated by two strangers inside her own home, Diana chooses to keep her trauma a secret. Mario, her husband, has something to hide as well. In the days that follow, the silence that grows between the two of them becomes its own kind of violence.
Shattered and glassy-eyed, the young mother of two, Diana, lies on the floor of her bedroom, stunned after the brutal assault by two rapists in her house. In the aftermath of this harrowing experience, Diana will call her husband, Mario, however, not to tell him what happened, but to have him pick up their kids from school, as she decides to keep her ordeal secret. But for how long will Diana cover up the truth? After all, Mario already senses that something is utterly wrong, moreover, if he ever finds out, to what extremes is a timid and hesitant man willing to go to protect his family?
Diana is raped in her own home but does not tell her husband, Mario. Mario witnessed part of the attack but does not intervene and does not tell Diana he was there. The film follows the ensuing weeks in their relationship, slowly feeding us clues to explain their actions and the two-year break in their marriage, while they try to keep things as normal as possible for their children. Mario, however, learns the identity of the rapists and seeks revenge while confronting his own demons. A film about love, courage and cactus.

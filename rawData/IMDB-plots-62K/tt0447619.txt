In France, the single translator Diane Siprien adopts an Asian baby named Liu-San in a foundation directed by Sybille Weber. Years later, a weird mark appears on the boy's chest and Diane and Liu share their dreadful nightmares. Diane is assigned for a three-day job in German and she leaves Liu with her friend Sybille. However, while going to the airport, Diane finds Liu hidden in the backseat and startles with an eagle flying toward the windshield, crashing her car. Liu falls into a coma and his digital recorder records the boy speaking in an unknown dialect. When Diane searches the translation and the origins of Liu, she is surrounded by mysterious murders. She discovers that the dialect is from the mystic Mongolian Tseven tribe and that Liu is a powerful Observer; further, he is in danger, threatened by sorcerers that need the boy for their Council of the Stone.
Single and attractive Laura Siprien adopts young Mongloid Liu-San and takes him home to her apartment in France. A few years later, she takes him for a medical examination after she finds a bruise on his chest. The examining doctor assures her that the bruise is not serious and will disappear in due course. When she decides to travel, she leaves Liu-San with her friend, Sybille, but finds out he has smuggled himself in the backseat of her car. It is after this discovery that her life will be shattered, and Liu-San will be abducted and taken to a secret location in Mongolia where a cult will prepare to sacrifice him in order to attain immortality.
Diane Siprien is a french scientist who adopts a young thai boy. After a car crash, this one falls into a coma and a mysterious german doctor asserts he can heal him... through unconventional methods! It leads Diane to Mongolia to find out the origin of the young boy.

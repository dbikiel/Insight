The Rule of Accident is about a group of theatre students who are trying to prepare and present a piece of theatre.
A group of young actors want to take their friend along with them to perform overseas. But the girl's overprotective father is afraid that she'll never return, or she'll try to kill herself again. The conflict in Behzadi's film is cross generational. Winner of the Special Jury Prize at the Tokyo International Film Festival, Bending the Rules portrays the conflict between young people and an older generation that fears the consequences of confrontation.
A group of young theater students rehearse for a play they will perform at an upcoming festival. As their individual personal issues arise, a new group dynamic develops. With laughter and tears they share their troubles and experiences. Through the strength of their friendship, they learn to overcome their obstacles together. 'The Rule of Accident' is a touching story about another kind of family.

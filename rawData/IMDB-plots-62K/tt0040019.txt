School for Postmen" is a 1947 short film directed and starring Jacques Tati, playing a French postman adamant to prove he can be just as fast as American postmen at delivering mail.
A French village postman (Jacques Tati) has to adapt to the new technology - air mail!. He has to speed up his rounds to meet a new, tighter schedule, greet his customers, deal with a bicycle with a mind of its own, and of course have a drink and a dance in the village pub. More brilliant physical comedy form the great Tati in a short film. This disc is included as an extra in Criteria's release on "Mon Oncle".

A talented high school lacrosse player deals with the pain from his military dad's death, a resentful new coach, and consequences of his own arrogance.
In shock and denial over his Marine father's death in battle, star lacrosse player Conor Sullivan, always a maverick and a hothead, starts acting out in self-destructive ways that have his mom, Claire, at her wit's end, but arduous training in a wilderness lacrosse camp under the tutelage of his dad's old combat buddy, Sgt. Major Duke Wayne, opens Conor's eyes to the true meaning of maturity, sportsmanship and manhood.
His Marine father killed in the line of duty, hotshot lacrosse player Conor Sullivan (Kellan Lutz) turns self-destructive. When Conor's volatile behavior lands him in the Six Nations Work Camp, his father's old military buddy Sgt. Major Duke Wayne (Adam Beach) offers the wayward youth a chance at redemption.
